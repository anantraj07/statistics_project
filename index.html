WHO Healthy Diet · January 2026

The Four Pillars of a Healthy Diet

WHO's core framework: Adequacy · Balance · Moderation · Diversity. Every recommendation on this platform traces back to these four principles.

⚖
Adequacy
Meets micronutrient and macronutrient needs without excess. Prevents deficiencies in iron, zinc, vitamin A, folate — the four most prevalent globally.
🔄
Balance
Total energy intake equals energy expenditure. Carbs 45–75%, Fats 15–30%, Protein 10–15% of total daily energy — no single macro dominates.
🎯
Moderation
Limit saturated fat <10%, trans fat <1%, free sugars <10%, salt <5g/day. Not zero — just limited. Deprivation causes diet failure.
🌈
Diversity
Wide variety within and across food groups. Dark greens for iron & vitamin A. Citrus for vitamin C. Dairy/fish for calcium. Pulses for fibre.

Carbohydrates
45–75% of calories

Primary energy source. Choose whole grains, oats, millets, pulses. Avoid refined flour and white rice as main source. 1g carb = 4 kcal.

🇮🇳 Indian sources: brown rice, ragi, bajra, jowar, whole wheat atta, lentils, chana dal
💪
Protein
10–15% of calories

Builds muscle, enzymes, hormones. ICMR: 0.8–1g/kg body weight. Athletes need 1.6–2.2g/kg. 1g protein = 4 kcal.

🇮🇳 Indian sources: dal, chana, rajma, soya, paneer, eggs, fish, chicken, curd
🥑
Fats
15–30% of calories

Essential for hormones, brain, fat-soluble vitamins. Choose unsaturated (mustard oil, nuts, seeds). Limit ghee, butter. 1g fat = 9 kcal.

🇮🇳 Indian sources: mustard oil, sesame, groundnuts, almonds, flaxseed, coconut
✨
Micronutrients
30+ vitamins & minerals

Iron, zinc, vitamin A, folate — most deficient in India. 50%+ of Indian women are anaemic. Requires diverse, colourful eating.

🇮🇳 Critical: spinach/iron, amla/vitamin C, carrots/vitamin A, milk/calcium, dal/zinc
For Absolute Beginners

Your Diet in Simple Terms

Before the calculator, understand these 5 concepts. Everything else follows from them.

01

Calories = Energy

Your body burns a certain number of calories per day just to exist (BMR) plus whatever you move. Eat less → lose weight. Eat more → gain weight. But quality matters — 500 kcal of dal is very different from 500 kcal of biscuits.

02

Protein is non-negotiable

Protein preserves muscle when losing weight and builds it when gaining. Most Indians eat 30–40g/day. You need 50–80g minimum. Dal at every meal + paneer/egg/fish is the minimum viable protein strategy.

03

Don't fear carbs, fear processed carbs

Roti, dal, rice — these are fine. Maida biscuits, white bread, packed snacks — avoid these. Fibre in whole grains keeps you full and feeds your gut microbiome.

04

Micronutrients prevent disease

You can have enough calories and still be malnourished. Iron deficiency causes fatigue and anaemia. Vitamin A deficiency causes night blindness. Eat vegetables of 3+ colours every day.

05

Timing matters less than totals

Daily totals > meal timing for most people. That said: eat breakfast (kickstarts metabolism), protein at every meal (muscle protein synthesis), avoid large meals 2h before bed (digestion quality).
